Our
History

How we Started

The museum was founded by John Smith in 1960. He was a resident of Stratford back when it was a small , unknown town in the province of Ontario. Him and his team built the museum from the ground up in order to bring more awareness to the beauty the town of Stratford had to offer. Over the next 5 years before the opening, he partnered with art museums all around Canada in order to fill his museum with the most beautiful artwork. After 2 decades TSAG began to grow and attract more attention from galleries all around the world and now become what it is today.

Today

The Stratford Art Gallery (TSAG), located in Stratford, Ontario, Canada opened its doors on September 16, 1965. Know for its wide variety of paintings, TSAG has been collecting famous art pieces for decades in order to build an incredible collection for you, your family and friends to enjoy. Director and CEO Jayna Patel has been with the museum for over 20 years sustaining the gallery through the 21st century. With over 3,000 pieces of artwork, TSAG encourages creativity through beautiful and rare paintings and sculptures. Our newly addition to TSAG is The Stratford Gardens which features real life representations of famous garden paintings such as Monet. This exhibit is only located 10 minutes from TSAG and is included in a day pass ticket!

Being located in Stratford, Ontario brings people from all over the world. We encourage a safe and inclusive viewing environment so that everyone has the opportunity to witness and experience traditional art like they have never seen before.
